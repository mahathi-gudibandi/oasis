Tribute to Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India | Former President of India

About

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, also known as the "Missile Man of India," was one of the most respected leaders and scientists of India. Born on October 15, 1931, he made significant contributions to the Indian space research and defense programs. He served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007 and was loved by millions for his humility, vision, and dedication to education and youth.

Achievements

  • Played a key role in India’s Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.
  • Developed ballistic missiles under the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (IGMDP).
  • Awarded the Bharat Ratna, India’s highest civilian award.
  • Inspired millions of students through his books like "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds".

“Dream, Dream, Dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action.” – A.P.J. Abdul Kalam
